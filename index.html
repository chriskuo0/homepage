Christopher Kuo

Postdoctoral Fellow
Max Planck Institute for Mathematics
chrislpkuo(at)berkeley{dot}edu

I am a postdoc at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics. Previously, I was a postdoc at the University of Southern California. My Ph.D. advisor was Vivek Shende. I am on the job market for both tenure-track and postdoctoral positions this year. My research interests include microlocal sheaf theory, noncommutative geometry, symplectic geometry, and D-modules. Here is my CV.

Research

  • Integral transforms and compactness, in preparation. Joint work with Sheel Ganatra , Wenyuan Li, and Haosen Wu.
  • C0-rigidity of Legendrians and coisotropics via sheaf quantization. Joint work with Tomohiro Asano, Yuichi Ike, Wenyuan Li.
  • Tropical Lagrangian coamoebae and free resolutions. Joint work with Harold Williams.
  • Relative Calabi-Yau structure on microlocalization. Joint work with Wenyuan Li.
  • Duality and Kernels in Microlocal Geometry, IMRN 2025 (2025): rnaf070. Joint work with Wenyuan Li. (arXiv:2405.15211.)
  • The microlocal Riemann-Hilbert correspondence for complex contact manifolds. Joint work with Laurent Côté, David Nadler, and Vivek Shende.
  • On the Hochschild cohomology of Tamarkin categories. Joint work with Vivek Shende and Bingyu Zhang.
  • Spherical adjunction and Serre functor from microlocalization. Joint work with Wenyuan Li. Notes for talks about this paper can be found here.
  • Perverse Microsheaves. Joint work with Laurent Côté, David Nadler, and Vivek Shende.
  • Symplectic geometric methods in microlocal sheaf theory. My Ph.D. thesis, a combniation of my paper, wrapped sheaves, and partial contents from my joint paper with Wenyuan Li, Duality and spherical adjunction from microlocalization, before the forming of our collaboration.
  • Wrapped sheaves, Adv. Math 415 (2023): 108882. (arXiv:2102.06791.) Notes for a talk given in USC is vailable here.
